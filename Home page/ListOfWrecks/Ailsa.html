S.S. AILSA

Shipwreck Details

Vessel Type: Steamer Coaster

Date of Loss: 26th February 1892

Cause of Loss: Ran aground

Depth: 5m

Wreck Condition: Scattered

Hull Material: Iron

Seabed Type: Rocks, Gravel & Mud

Visibility: 5 - 10m

Location: North of Portmuck, Co. Antrim

Boat Dive from: Ballylumford

GPS Coordinates: 54° 51' 12.24" N, 05° 44' 18.12" W

Diving Experience: Novice

Diving Information

  • Lies 0.75 miles North of Portmuck.
  • Badly broken up in 3 - 5m of water.
  • Part of the hull can be seen above the high water mark.
  • Launch from Ballylumford Harbour anytime or Portmuck at high tide.

Historical Information

  • Originally built as the "Rose" in 1867 by Blackwood & Gordon, Glasgow.
  • Weighed 185 net tons with dimensions of 54.99m x 7.36m x 4.16m.
  • Owned by Ayr Steam Shipping Co., carrying cargo and one passenger when it ran aground.
