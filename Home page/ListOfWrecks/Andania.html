S.S. ANDANIA

Shipwreck Details

Vessel Type: Armed Passenger Liner

Date of Loss: 27th January 1918

Cause of Loss: Torpedoed

Charted Depth: 123m

Height of Wreck: 13m

Hull Material: Steel

Seabed Type: Sand over clay

Visibility: 1 - 2m

Location: Co. Antrim

Place: Between Rathlin & Mull of Kintyre

Boat Dive from: Ballycastle

GPS Coordinates: 55° 25' 40.8" N, 06° 17' 37.2" W

Diving Experience: Specialist Diving

Diving Information

  • Lies 2 miles North-North-East of Altacarry Lighthouse.
  • Wreck lies across the tide.
  • Bow is damaged.
  • A Tri-Mix dive for the very experienced.
  • Tide is extremely strong and visibility is usually almost zero.

Historical Information

  • Built by Scotts of Greenock in 1913, owned by Cunard Line.
  • Grossed 13,405 tons and measured 520 x 64 x 43ft.
  • Armed with a 1 x 4 inch stern gun.
  • Torpedoed by U-46 while 2 miles NNE of Altacarry Lighthouse.
  • Seven crew died.
  • Rest of the crew were rescued by nearby vessels.
  • Attempts to take her in tow were quickly abandoned as she sank rapidly.
